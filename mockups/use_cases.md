### Phase 13: Accessibility, Comfort & Sensory Control

*Solving the physical and auditory friction of watching.*

**57. Scenario: The "Volume Yo-Yo"**

* **The Problem:** The dialogue is whispering, so you turn the volume up to 60. Suddenly, a car explodes and wakes up the entire neighborhood.
* **The Feature: "Night Mode Audio EQ."** CINESCOPE connects to your smart TV/soundbar and actively normalizes the audio mixing in real-time, compressing the dynamic range so dialogue and explosions sit at the exact same decibel level.

**58. Scenario: The "Punchline Ruined by Subtitles"**

* **The Problem:** You are watching a comedy, and the subtitle reveals the joke's punchline three seconds before the actor actually says it.
* **The Feature: "Comedic Timing Subtitles."** A specialized subtitle setting where the text is revealed word-by-word matching the audio track, preserving dramatic pauses and comedic timing perfectly.

**59. Scenario: The "Where Am I Looking?" Distraction**

* **The Problem:** Subtitles are often placed right over crucial visual information, or they are just an ugly, blocky white font.
* **The Feature: "Dynamic Placement Captions."** The app uses AI to analyze the visual frame and automatically shifts the subtitles to the negative space on the screen, ensuring faces and crucial actions are never covered up.

**60. Scenario: The "Epilepsy / Migraine Trigger"**

* **The Problem:** You suffer from migraines or epilepsy, and a sudden strobe-light scene in a sci-fi movie ruins your night.
* **The Feature: "Sensory Safe Mode."** The app cross-references a database of visual triggers. As a high-contrast or strobe scene approaches, the app automatically dims your screen to 10% brightness and mutes the audio until the scene passes.

**61. Scenario: The "I Missed What They Just Said"**

* **The Problem:** You missed a line of dialogue, but when you hit rewind on the TV remote, it jumps back 30 seconds and you have to sit through a whole scene again.
* **The Feature: "The 'What Was That?' Button."** A single button on the app remote that instantly rewinds exactly 10 seconds and *temporarily* turns on subtitles for those 10 seconds, then turns them back off automatically.
